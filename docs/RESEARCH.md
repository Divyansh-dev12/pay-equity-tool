## ACADEMIC FOUNDATIONS

### #1: Oaxaca (1973) — "Male-Female Wage Differentials in Urban Labor Markets"

**Citation:**  
Oaxaca, R. (1973). "Male-Female Wage Differentials in Urban Labor Markets." *International Economic Review*, 14(3): 693–709.

**Links:**
- [JSTOR](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2525981) (paywalled)
- [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/222242826_On_Discrimination_and_the_Decomposition_of_Wage_Differentials) (free)
- [IZA](https://docs.iza.org/dp16188.pdf)

**Why it matters:**
Foundational paper on wage decomposition. Divides wage differences between two groups into:
- **Explained part:** Due to differences in productivity characteristics (education, experience, job level)
- **Unexplained residual:** Cannot be accounted for by measurable factors

**Key insight for our framework:**
This is the methodology behind "controlled vs. uncontrolled gaps." Your Step 3 analysis directly implements Oaxaca's decomposition approach.

**Use in presentations:**
"The Oaxaca decomposition, used since 1973, separates the portion of the wage gap due to different job roles/levels (which is not discrimination) from the portion unexplained by those factors (which may indicate bias)."

### #2: Blau & Kahn (2017) — "The Gender Wage Gap: Extent, Trends, and Explanations"

**Citation:**  
Blau, F. D., & Kahn, L. M. (2017). "The Gender Wage Gap: Extent, Trends, and Explanations." *Journal of Economic Literature*, 55(3): 789–865.

**Links:**
- [American Economic Association](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257%2Fjel.20160995) (full text)
- [NBER Working Paper](https://www.nber.org/papers/w21913) (free version)

**Data:**
Uses Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID) microdata covering 1980–2010 (30 years).

**Key findings:**
1. **Occupational segregation is the major driver** of gender wage gaps, not just pay discrimination
2. By 2010, conventional human capital variables (education, experience) explained **very little** of the gap
3. **Glass ceiling effect:** Gender pay gaps are much worse at the top of the wage distribution than at the middle or bottom
4. Progress in closing gaps has been slow in recent decades

**Implication:**
Even if your company pays men and women the same within roles (no discrimination), if women are concentrated in lower-paying roles, you'll still have a large overall gap. This is why Step 2 in our framework distinguishes between "unadjusted gap" and "controlled gap."

**Use in presentations:**
"Research by Blau & Kahn shows that occupational segregation—women in lower-paying roles—accounts for more of the wage gap than differences in pay decisions. Our framework separates these effects."

## CORPORATE CASE STUDIES

### #3: Salesforce Pay Equity Initiative (2015–Present)

**Overview:**
One of the first major companies to publicly evaluate and address pay gaps at scale.

**Key Case Study Links:**
- [Syndio case study](https://synd.io/case-study/salesforce-scales-pay-equity-using-syndio/)
- [Salesforce official story](https://www.salesforce.com/news/stories/our-pay-philosophy/)
- [2022 update](https://www.salesforce.com/news/stories/2022-equal-pay-update-the-salesforce-approach-to-pay-fairness/)
- [Mason Frank analysis](https://www.masonfrank.com/insights/salesforce-leading-the-way-women-in-tech)

**By the numbers:**
- **2015:** Initial audit found $6 million pay disparity across 35,000 employees → 5% required adjustments
- **2019:** Continued audits; down to 5% of employees needing adjustments
- **2022:** Audited 70,000 global employees (post-acquisition); 8.5% required adjustments
  - 92% based on gender globally
  - 8% based on race/ethnicity in U.S.
  - **Spent $5.6 million in 2022 alone; $22 million total since 2015**
- **Ongoing:** Annual audits are standard practice

**Why it matters:**
- **Proves annual audits are necessary** — gaps re-emerge as new hires, promotions, transfers happen
- **Shows real cost** — $5-6M/year is material but manageable for a large org
- **Demonstrates compound effect** — improvements compound; earlier action = faster convergence
- **CEO commitment critical** — Marc Benioff made this a public priority, building employee trust

**Use in case studies/presentations:**
"Salesforce spent $22 million fixing pay equity over 7 years. Google settled for $118 million for the same issue. The difference: Salesforce audited proactively."

### #4: Microsoft Diversity & Inclusion Reporting (2013–Present)

**Official Reports:**
- [Microsoft Gender Pay Gap Report](https://www.microsoft.com/en-gb/about/gender-pay-gap/)
- [10-year transparency analysis (Forbes)](https://www.forbes.com/sites/carolinamilanesi/2023/11/02/microsoft-marks-10-years-of-dei-data-with-latest-report/)

**Key findings (2023):**
- **Controlled gaps are near zero:** All racial/ethnic minorities and women earn comparable total pay to counterparts with same job title, level, and tenure
- **Unadjusted gap narrowed:** Progress on women in U.S., women outside U.S., Asian/Black/Hispanic employees
- **Leadership pipeline:** Women in executive roles increased to 29.1% (3.2 point year-over-year growth)

**Why it matters:**
- Shows it's **possible to achieve zero controlled gap** at scale
- Demonstrates that transparency + public commitment drives change
- But also note: Only 11% of companies studied achieved similar results

**Caveat (Harvard Business School research):**
Companies that **talk more about diversity** often have **larger pay gaps**. Talk without action is counterproductive.

## LEGAL / REGULATORY CASE STUDIES

### #5A: Google Pay Equity Settlement (2022)

**Settlement details:**
- [Lieff Cabraser announcement](https://www.lieffcabraser.com/2022/06/plaintiffs-and-google-agree-to-118-million-settlement-of-pay-equity-class-action/)

**By the numbers:**
- **Settlement amount:** $118 million
- **Employees affected:** ~15,500 women across 236 different job titles
- **Average underpayment:** ~$16,794 per year per woman
- **Basis:** Claims filed under California's Equal Pay Act

**Timeline:**
The case lasted several years, involving:
- Discovery of internal emails
- Expert economic analysis
- Testimony from affected employees
- Negotiation + settlement

**Why it matters:**
- **Reactive cost >> Proactive cost:** $118M settlement vs. ~$5-6M/year for proactive audits
- Shows what happens when you avoid auditing
- Demonstrates litigation risk: reputational damage + discovery process
- Internal emails often become damaging evidence (documenting bias)

**Use in presentations:**
"Google settled pay equity claims for $118 million. Salesforce fixed gaps for $5-6 million per year. The difference is auditing proactively."

### #5B: Oracle Pay Discrimination Litigation (2017–Present)

**SEC Filings:**
- [2017 filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001341439/000139834417013971/fp0028931_px14a6g.htm)
- [2019 filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001341439/000139834419020291/fp0047662_px14a6g.htm)
- [2020 filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001341439/000139834420020816/fp0058856_px14a6g.htm)

**By the numbers:**
- **Alleged by DOL:** Women in Product Development, IT, and Support underpaid up to 20%, affecting 5,000+ women
- **Also alleged:** Asian employees underpaid by up to 8% vs. white employees, affecting 11,000+ employees
- **Class action:** 4,000+ women software engineers sued in 2020 (achieved class-action status in April 2020)

**Litigation status:**
Ongoing as of 2024; multiple simultaneous proceedings (DOL investigation + class actions)

**Why it matters:**
- Shows what happens when you **resist disclosure + avoid audits**
- **Dual litigation:** Both government (DOL) + private (class action) simultaneously
- 20% gap in tech roles is extraordinarily high (vs. 5-8% industry average)
- Long legal discovery process = years of diversion + expense

## RESEARCH: "Talk ≠ Action"

**Study:**  
[Harvard Business School Research](https://www.library.hbs.edu/working-knowledge/more-talk-less-action-companies-touting-gender-equity-often-have-larger-pay-gaps)

**Surprising finding:**
**Companies that talk MORE about diversity initiatives often have WORSE gender pay gaps.**

**Implication:**
- Words without action are perceived as performative ("diversity theater")
- Can signal to external observers that company is aware of gaps but not fixing them
- May actually increase reputational risk if gaps are discovered later

**For your framework:**
This emphasizes the importance of Step 5 (Remediation). Auditing + action is credible. Auditing without remediation looks worse than not auditing at all.
